What is the flow of the traffic?

This question refers to the direction of network traffic. Depending by this flow risks can increase drastically.

Scoring

The flows have the following score:

  • Inbound from internet - 20
  • Outbound to internet - 15
  • Internal cross-segment - 10
  • Internal inter-segment - 5

Risks

Depending by the flow of the traffic, it might be exposes the following risks:

  • Unauthorized access
  • Data exfiltration
  • Malware propagation
  • DDoS attacks
  • Data interception

What type of data is involved?

This question refers to the type of data that is involved in the network traffic. Depending by the data type risks can increase drastically.

Scoring

The data types have the following score:

  • Sensitive data (e.g., PII, financial) - 20
  • Important data (e.g., internal business data) - 15
  • General data (e.g., non-sensitive internal data) - 10
  • Non-sensitive data - 5

Risks

Depending by the data type, it might be exposes the following risks:

  • Data exfiltration
  • Unauthorized access
  • Data interception

What is the destination/source of the traffic?

Scoring

The system types have the following score:

  • Critical systems (e.g., databases, application servers) - 20
  • Important systems (e.g., file servers, internal applications) - 15
  • General systems (e.g., user workstations) - 10
  • Low-sensitivity systems (e.g., printers, VoIP phones) - 5

What protocol is used?

Scoring

Depending of type of protocol used, we have the following score:

  • Unencrypted protocols (e.g., Telnet, FTP) - 20
  • Secure protocols (e.g., SSH, HTTPS) - 10
  • Internal encrypted protocols (e.g., internal VPN) - 5

What compensating controls are in place?

Some of the risk might be mitigated by compensating controls.

Scoring

Depending of type of compensating controls, we have the following score:

  • None - 15
  • Local Logging - 10
  • Advanced logging, monitoring and IDS/IPS - 5

How frequently is the rule used?

Logic: A rule that is used more often is both more critical and more prone to exploitation

Scoring

Depending of type of protocol used, we have the following score:

  • Very often (high volume daily) - 20
  • Constantly (average daily volume) - 15
  • Occasionally (used once a couple of days or very low daily traffic) - 10
  • Rarely (use spordaically) - 5
